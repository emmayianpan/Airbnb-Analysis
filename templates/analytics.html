According to Airbnb, listings are categorized into the following home types:

  • Entire Home/Apt: Guests have the whole place to themselves. This usually includes a bedroom, a bathroom, and a kitchen. Hosts should note in the description if they'll be on the property
  • Private Room: Guests have their own private room for sleeping. Other areas could be shared.
  • Shared Room: Guests sleep in a bedroom or a common area that could be shared with others.
  • Hotel Room: Listings are hosted by professional hospitality businesses that offer unique spaces and personal hospitality.

The chart shows that New York City (45,756 units) has highest numbers of total units, following by Los Angeles (31,536 units), Hawaii (22,434 units) and Broward County (10,858 units), which are all provided over 10,000 units.

Entire Home/Apt accounts for larger proportion in each city compared to other room types. Hawaii and Nashville have the highest percentage of Entire Home/Apt, ~89% and ~87% respectively, but with the lower percentage of Private Room, ~9% and ~12% respectively. However, New York City has the lowest percentage of Entire Home/Apt (~52%), but with highest percentage of Private Room (~46%). The second proportion in each city is Private Room and the combination of Hotel Room and Shared Room take account for no more than 4% for ten cities.



The chart displays with a descending order by average price of each city, referred to left-sided y-axis. Total Supply is referred to right-sided y-axis.


The chart shows that city with the highest average price Columbus, following by Hawaii, San Francisco, Broward County. However, New York City is the least average price among ten cities. The given data provides an insight/assumption of the correlations between supply and price tag, which might relate to supply/demand situation on some cities. For example, Columbus and New York City have the highest and lowest average price respectively but the opposite situation on total supply.
